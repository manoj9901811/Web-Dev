Frequently Asked Questions

What is HDPE and why is it used for pipes?

High-Density Polyethylene (HDPE) is a thermoplastic polymer known for its high strength-to-density ratio. It's used for pipes because it resists corrosion, chemicals, and UV radiation while remaining flexible — making it ideal for water supply, gas distribution, and industrial applications.

What are the benefits of using HDPE pipes in manufacturing?

HDPE pipes offer superior chemical resistance, a 50+ year service life, leak-proof fusion joints, lower installation costs, and environmental sustainability compared to PVC or metal alternatives.

How are HDPE pipes produced?

Production follows a multi-stage extrusion process: raw PE100 resin is melted, extruded through a die, cooled in vacuum sizing tanks for precise dimensional control, then marked and cut to specification.

What are the common applications of HDPE pipes?

HDPE pipes are used in potable water distribution, irrigation, natural gas pipelines, sewage and drainage systems, industrial process piping, mining slurry lines, and marine applications.

Can HDPE pipes be customized?

Yes — we offer custom diameters (20mm–1600mm), wall thicknesses (SDR 11–33), pressure ratings (PN 2.5–16), coil lengths, and colors. Contact our engineers for tailored specifications.

Advanced HDPE Pipe Manufacturing Process

Our state-of-the-art extrusion technology ensures consistent quality, optimal material properties, and dimensional accuracy in every pipe we manufacture.

High-Grade Raw Material Selection

Vacuum sizing tanks ensure precise outer diameter while internal pressure maintains perfect roundness and wall thickness uniformity.

  • PE100 grade material
  • Optimal molecular weight distribution
  • Colour masterbatch integration

Precision Extrusion

Single-screw extruders with precise temperature control zones melt and homogenise the resin before forming the pipe profile.

  • Consistent melt temperature
  • Auto-correcting die heads

Controlled Cooling

Multi-stage water bath cooling solidifies the pipe at a controlled rate to prevent residual stress and ensure dimensional stability.

  • Gradual thermal quench
  • Stress-free pipe wall

Vacuum Sizing

Precision vacuum sizing tanks lock in the final outer diameter while internal air pressure maintains wall uniformity.

  • ±0.1mm diameter tolerance
  • Uniform wall thickness

Quality Control

In-line and off-line testing per IS 5984, ISO 4427, and ASTM D3035 including hydrostatic pressure tests and dimensional checks.

  • 100% hydrostatic testing
  • Tensile & impact tests

Marking & Identification

Continuous inkjet printing marks every pipe with product code, diameter, SDR rating, pressure class, and production batch for full traceability.

  • ISO-compliant marking
  • Batch traceability

Precision Cutting

Automated planetary cutters produce clean, square ends suitable for immediate fusion jointing without additional preparation.

  • Burr-free end cuts
  • Custom lengths available

Packaging & Dispatch
